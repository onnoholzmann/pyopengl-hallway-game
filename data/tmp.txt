This is a classic OpenGL coordinate system gotcha 👍

💥 Why it only shows bottom-left

This line is the culprit:

glOrtho(0, W, 0, H, -1, 1)

👉 In OpenGL, this means:

(0, 0) = BOTTOM-LEFT

(W, H) = TOP-RIGHT

But in pygame:

(0, 0) = TOP-LEFT

y increases downwards

🔥 So what’s happening?

When you do:

glRasterPos2f(x, y)

👉 you're placing text using bottom-left origin, not pygame's top-left.

So your (200, 200) is actually:

200 px right ✅

200 px UP from bottom, not down from top ❌

✅ Fix #1 (recommended): flip Y axis

Change your projection to match pygame:

glOrtho(0, W, H, 0, -1, 1)

👉 Now:

(0, 0) = TOP-LEFT

(W, H) = BOTTOM-RIGHT

✅ Fix #2: adjust manually

If you keep your current projection, you must flip y:

glRasterPos2f(x, H - y)
⚠️ You also have a bug here

Inside your class:

glRasterPos2f(x, y)

👉 x and y are undefined.

You should use:

x, y = self.location
glRasterPos2f(x, y)
✅ Final corrected function
def draw_opengl(self, screen):
    text_data = pygame.image.tostring(self.surface, "RGBA", True)
    w, h = self.surface.get_size()
    x, y = self.location

    glMatrixMode(GL_PROJECTION)
    glPushMatrix()
    glLoadIdentity()
    glOrtho(0, W, H, 0, -1, 1)  # 🔥 flipped Y

    glMatrixMode(GL_MODELVIEW)
    glPushMatrix()
    glLoadIdentity()

    glDisable(GL_DEPTH_TEST)
    glUseProgram(0)
    glEnable(GL_BLEND)
    glBlendFunc(GL_SRC_ALPHA, GL_ONE_MINUS_SRC_ALPHA)

    glRasterPos2f(x, y)
    glDrawPixels(w, h, GL_RGBA, GL_UNSIGNED_BYTE, text_data)

    glDisable(GL_BLEND)
    glEnable(GL_DEPTH_TEST)

    glMatrixMode(GL_PROJECTION)
    glPopMatrix()
    glMatrixMode(GL_MODELVIEW)
    glPopMatrix()
